Services

This page helps visitors self-select before calling. Browse common services, see typical booking reasons, and (below) check each provider’s focus areas.

Availability varies by provider and schedule. If you’re unsure what to book, contact the clinic and we’ll help.

Common services

High-level overview. Each card should answer: “Is this something you handle?” and “What do I do next?”

Primary care

Annual visits · ongoing support · follow-ups

Routine visits, preventive planning, and long-term health support. If you’re establishing care, start with a new patient appointment.

Preventive care

Screenings · immunizations · wellness

Support for staying ahead of problems with practical next steps. We’ll help you understand what screenings may be appropriate for your age and history.

Sick visits

Short-term concerns · symptom support

When something feels off and you want guidance. Same-week availability varies—call to confirm openings. For severe symptoms, go to urgent care or the ER.

Chronic care

Ongoing conditions · monitoring · care plans

Structured follow-ups for ongoing conditions. Visits focus on practical, manageable plans and clear “what to do next” guidance.

Women’s / Men’s health

Routine care · preventive support

Routine visits and ongoing support tailored to your needs. If you’re unsure what to book, contact the clinic and we’ll route you appropriately.

Telehealth (if offered)

When appropriate · follow-ups

Virtual visits can be a good fit for select concerns and follow-ups. Some issues require an in-person exam—staff will help determine the best option.

What patients commonly book for

This section reduces calls by helping people describe what they need. Keep it short and non-diagnostic.

New patient visit

Establish care · transfer records

First visit at the clinic, care history review, and planning. Bring a medication list and any records you have.

Annual / wellness visit

Preventive planning

A check-in focused on prevention and long-term health. Staff can clarify what to expect and what to bring.

Follow-up

Results · ongoing care

Review labs, progress, or next steps. Great for chronic care check-ins and medication adjustments when needed.

Short-term symptoms

Sick visit

New symptoms or concerns you’d like evaluated. If symptoms are severe, go to urgent care or the ER.

Medication questions

Refills · side effects

Questions about current medications, refills, or side effects. Policies vary—ask staff what timing to expect.

Preventive items

Immunizations · screenings

Vaccines, screening guidance, and prevention planning. If you’re unsure what applies to you, we’ll help.
